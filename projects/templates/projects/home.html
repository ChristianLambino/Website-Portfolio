Fig. 01 — About

Developing for the fortieth screen, not just the first.

Introducing myself and my approach being a Full Stack Website Developer and UI/UX designer.

I'm a Full Stack Website Developer and UI/UX designer based in Pangasinan, working with teams who need interfaces that make sense at scale. My background spans at intern in Air Force building a Full System, inventory and ticketing system, to a freelance designer and developer building websites and dashboards for startups and operations teams.

I care most about the unglamorous parts of a product: empty states, edge cases, settings panels, and the fortieth screen of a dashboard nobody designed because "there wasn't time." Those are usually where a product earns trust — or loses it.

Day to day, that means moving between research, interface design, and the design-system thinking that keeps everything consistent as a team and product grow past the first version.

Fig. 05 — Process

How a project usually moves.

Four stages, roughly in this order — though real projects loop back.

01

Discover

Talk to users and stakeholders, audit what exists, and define the real problem before touching a screen.

02

Define

Map flows, structures, and edge cases — deciding what the system needs to support, not just the happy path.

03

Design

Build the interface and the components behind it together, so the UI and the system grow from one source.

04

Deliver

Hand off detailed specs, prototypes, and documentation so the system holds together after launch.
